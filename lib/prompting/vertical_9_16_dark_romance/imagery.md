# Image Generation: Vertical Dark Romance Grid

## Grid Layout
Single 2K vertical image with a 3×3 grid of 9 panels.

## Technical Specs
- **Aspect Ratio**: 9:16 vertical, 3×3 grid
- **Resolution**: 2K
- **Panels**: 9 (read left-to-right, top-to-bottom — Panel 1 opens, Panel 9 closes)

## Panel Organization
A single vertical 3×3 grid. The panels represent a continuous emotional sequence within one location. The entire grid tells one charged encounter — from composed restraint to barely-contained desire and back.

## Composition Rules
1. **Verticality as Intimacy**: Use the 9:16 frame to emphasize height differential, proximity, and the space — or absence of space — between faces. The vertical line of a body, a doorframe, a glass held at chest height: these are natural structural elements.
2. **Proximity Progression**: The 9-panel arc should show physical and emotional proximity changing — distance in Panel 1 becoming something else by Panel 9. Plan each panel's interpersonal distance deliberately.
3. **Rule of Thirds + Safe Zone**: Key subjects (faces, hands with props) in the central 65% of frame height. Top 15% and bottom 20% clear for text overlay.

## Visual Consistency
- **Lighting**: Single motivated practical source per scene — candle, floor lamp, city window. Must remain consistent across all 9 panels. Shadows fall in the same direction in every panel of the scene.
- **Camera**: Feels like a single 85mm lens used throughout. Angle changes for dramatic effect, but focal length consistency maintained — background bokeh stays the same depth and quality.
- **Character Consistency**: Same face, same clothing, same hair across all panels. Expressions change; everything else is fixed.

## Dark Romance Cinematography Standard

### Proximity as Drama
The most powerful visual element is not action — it is **distance between bodies**.
- Panels 1–3: characters separated by a desk, a room, a social boundary
- Panels 4–6: that distance closing without acknowledgment
- Panels 7–9: proximity that cannot be explained by circumstance alone

### Touch Hierarchy (escalate deliberately across episodes, not within a single scene)
gaze → sustained gaze → breath proximity → incidental contact → deliberate contact → withdrawal

### Lighting for Character
- Male lead: harder light, more shadow, stronger jaw definition — authority and danger
- Female lead: slightly softer fill on the face, but never weak — composed, watchful
- When they share a frame: the light source favors the one with power in that panel

### Luxury Texture as Subtext
Render fabric, surface, and material with maximum detail at ECU:
- Cashmere under tension, silk catching light, polished marble cold under fingertips
- A luxury watch at the wrist, a crystal glass, a document on a desk — objects carry meaning
- The quality of things tells you about the person who owns them

## Extreme Close-Up (ECU) Protocol
Maximum facial detail — visible skin pores, micro-muscle tension, the specific weight of the eyelid.
- Jaw: the barely-visible clench of a held decision
- Eyes: one sharp catchlight. Iris detail. The direction of the gaze (not at the face — at the mouth, the collar, the hands)
- Lips: the fractional parting of breath held too long

## Color Grade Standard
- Base palette: midnight blue shadows, warm amber-burgundy midtones, gold highlights
- Skin tones: warm but real — no wax, no plastic, camera-ready glow
- Background: dark enough that faces are the only light source in the frame
- One accent per scene: a red detail, a gold object, a pale hand against dark cloth

## IMPORTANT
- **NO TEXT CAPTIONS/SUBTITLES** — the image is the text
- Render desire through control: not heaving bosoms but a jaw held too still, eyes that don't look away when they should
