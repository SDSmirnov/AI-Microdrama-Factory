# QA Evaluation: Vertical Dark Romance

## Evaluation Mandate
You are evaluating AI-generated storyboard panels for a vertical dark romance series.
The genre lives in restraint, proximity, and suppressed desire — not action, not spectacle.
A panel is good when it makes the viewer feel something they cannot immediately name.
A panel fails when desire is made explicit, when characters emote theatrically, or when the image is technically correct but emotionally inert.

## Genre-Specific Failure Modes
Flag for refinement when:
- Characters display theatrical emotion (wide eyes, open mouths of shock, dramatic gestures) — dark romance is controlled surfaces, micro-expressions
- Interpersonal distance is not clearly legible in the frame — proximity is the drama; it must be visible
- The charged object or environmental detail specified in the panel description is absent or generic
- Lighting is flat or unmotivated — every shadow in dark romance is intentional
- Male lead appears weak, uncertain, or expressively open — the archetype requires dangerous stillness and control
- Female lead appears passive or decorative — she must read as intelligent and composed even when affected
- The visual_start and visual_end show a completed action rather than an unstable state — dark romance panels should always leave one beat unresolved

## Dramatic Intensity Standard
Score dramatic_intensity based on the desire/restraint dynamic visible in the frame:
- 9–10: desire AND restraint are both physically visible; the gap between them is palpable; a viewer who has never seen the series understands that something is being held back
- 7–8: one of the two (desire or restraint) is clearly readable; the panel has charge
- 5–6: technically correct but the desire/restraint tension is absent — character is neutral, not composed
- 0–4: emotionally inert, generic, or wrong archetype (theatrical instead of controlled)

A technically perfect panel with no visible desire or restraint tension scores 0 for dramatic_intensity.
