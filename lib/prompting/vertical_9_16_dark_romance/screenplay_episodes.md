## VERTICAL DARK ROMANCE DRAMATURGY

**The 3-Second Visual Hook:** Episode opens on maximum sensory impact — a face too close, a hand on a surface that says everything, an object that shouldn't be here. Zero explanation. The viewer asks "what is this?" That question is the leash.

**The 7-Second Charged Line:** By the second panel (≈7s), a character speaks a line that is ambiguous enough to mean two things and charged enough to mean only one. NOT exposition. NOT conflict. A statement that recontextualizes the entire situation in 8 words or fewer. Examples: "You shouldn't be here." / "I've been thinking." / "Close the door." / "I know what you want."

**The 21-Second Acknowledgment:** By panel 4 (≈21s), the viewer witnesses the protagonist acknowledge — even only internally — that they are not in control of what they feel. An admission that cannot be taken back. The held breath. The eyes that track to the wrong place. A small physical betrayal that the character immediately suppresses. If the viewer has survived to panel 4, they are captured for the episode.

**Cold Open = Desire Before Context:** The cold_open is not threat — it is pull. Show the EFFECT before the CAUSE: she is already flustered before we see who caused it. He is already watching before we establish why. The reaction before the stimulus. The viewer must ask "what happened between them?" THAT question is the engine.

**Micro-Act Structure (per episode, 9 panels):**
- Panels 1–2: HOOK + CHARGE. Drop into intimate tension, then establish who and what.
- Panels 3–5: PROXIMITY. The space between them closes — physically, emotionally — without either acknowledging it.
- Panels 6–7: THRESHOLD. The moment where one more inch of proximity, one more second of eye contact, will make denial impossible.
- Panel 8: BREAK OR RETREAT. One character acts — or forces themselves not to. Both choices are devastating.
- Panel 9: CLIFFHANGER. The new status quo is unbearable. Something has changed that cannot be unchanged.

**Proximity Physics:** The drama lives in the gap. Plan each panel's interpersonal distance: far → socially appropriate → uncomfortably close → intimate → withdrawal. The withdrawal is the climax, not the approach.

**Dialogue Contract:** ≤8 words. Ambiguous. The spoken line and the subtext must point in opposite directions. What they say versus what they mean: the gap between those two things is the hook. Silence after a line is never empty — it is the viewer's pulse.

**Desire Through Control:** Never show desire directly. Show its suppression: the jaw that tightens, the glass gripped slightly too hard, the eye that tracks to the mouth and then back up, hoping it wasn't noticed. The control IS the desire. The moment the control slips — for one panel — is the episode's climax.

**Visual Motif — forbidden object:** In episode 1, establish one object that connects the two characters: a specific book, a ring, a pen, a piece of paper. Record it in visual_continuity_rules as "MOTIF: [description]". Call it back in the connection episode — same object, new meaning, everything changed.

**Cliffhanger = The New Impossible:** The final panel establishes a new reality that cannot be undone: she knows something she cannot unknow, he has shown something he cannot take back, they are alone in a way that changes every future interaction. The viewer must feel the unbearable weight of what just became possible.

## GOLDEN RULES OF DARK ROMANCE TEXT

* **Restraint Over Explosion:** The most erotic moment is the one before. The most devastating moment is the one after. Never land the punch — show the wind-up and the aftermath.
* **Physical Specificity:** Not "he looked at her" — write: "his gaze dropped to her hands, then returned to her face a half-second too late." The delay is the entire story.
* **The Other Character Is Always Present (Even Absent):** In POV-A (her perspective), his absence shapes every action. In POV-B (his perspective), she is in every thought. The viewer feels both presences simultaneously.
* **Power Is Never Static:** Who controls the scene shifts. She asks a question; he answers it with silence and reclaims control. Track the power shift in every panel — it is the real plot.
