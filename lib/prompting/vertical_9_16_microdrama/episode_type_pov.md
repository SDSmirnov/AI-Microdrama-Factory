## EPISODE TYPE: POV — SINGLE CHARACTER PERSPECTIVE (INTERIOR)
This episode shows events from the protagonist's point of view — their actions, interior thoughts, and immediate observations.
- SINGLE POV THROUGHOUT: only the protagonist's experience is depicted. Supporting characters are present but never given equal screen weight — they are obstacles, catalysts, or pressures on the protagonist.
- CAPTIVE AUDIENCE RULE: the viewer already knows the protagonist. Panel 1 does NOT re-introduce them. Drop into whatever has just escalated since the previous episode — the protagonist is already mid-situation.
- ESCALATION RULE: this episode must leave the protagonist in a more difficult position than when it began. Every panel increases pressure. No holding patterns.
- EPISODE ENDING: if this is an intermediate episode in the series — end on tension_peak (maximum escalation, no resolution, voiceover mandatory 4–5 words). If this is the final episode — end on cliffhanger (response pressure, protagonist frozen before their response).
