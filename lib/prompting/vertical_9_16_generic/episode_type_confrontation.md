## EPISODE TYPE: CONFRONTATION — TWO-CHARACTER DIRECT EXCHANGE
This episode centers on a direct exchange between two characters — argument, negotiation, confession, standoff, or confrontation.
- SPATIAL STAGING: establish the power relationship in the first panel through spatial position (who stands, who sits, who controls the door).
- ALTERNATING COVERAGE: cut between the two characters' faces as the exchange progresses. Neither character should dominate more than 60% of panels unless the source gives them that weight.
- DIALOGUE FIDELITY: every spoken line from the source must appear verbatim. Do not paraphrase, combine, or omit lines.
- REACTION SHOTS: every significant line delivered by one character must be followed by the other character's physical reaction before the scene moves on.
- EPISODE ENDING: end where the source text ends. If the confrontation resolves — show the resolution. If it ends unresolved — show the unresolved state. Do not invent a winner.
