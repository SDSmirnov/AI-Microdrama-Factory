## EPISODE TYPE: arc_close — FINAL EPISODE OF THE ARC UNIT

This is always the last episode of the arc unit (N=2 or N=3).
Single POV — same character perspective as all prior episodes in the unit.

**Arc position:** panels 10–18 (N=2) or 19–27 (N=3) of the full arc (labeled locally as P1–9).
**Dramatic function:** Deliver the confrontation the full arc was building, twist the knife, and freeze on the cliffhanger.

**Arc Pickup (mandatory, P1):** hook_type: arc_pickup.
Same location, same moment as the previous episode's arc_bridge, 1–2 seconds later.
In N=2: the pickup continues from arc_open's bridge — the energy must accelerate immediately.
In N=3: the pickup continues from arc_mid's bridge — the confrontation is already boiling; arrive at it fast.
A viewer starting here must read who this character is and what the immediate stakes are through visible action.

**Confrontation pacing by N:**
- N=2: P2=escalation_return, P3=confrontation_build, P4=confrontation_peak. Must compress 3 steps into panels 2–4.
- N=3: P2=confrontation_build, P3=confrontation_peak, P4=peak_intensity. Arrives at maximum conflict by P3.

**True Cliffhanger (mandatory, P9):** hook_type: cliffhanger.
- Freeze on maximum unresolved tension. End mid-breath.
- One visible element with two possible interpretations — the viewer rewinds because they missed something.
- Never resolve. Never summarize. This is the Button.

**Motif payoff (mandatory):**
The visual motif from arc_open MUST appear in P9 — same framing, same object or gesture, transformed meaning.
State in screenplay_instructions: "MOTIF CALLBACK: [original motif] — [transformed meaning in cliffhanger]."

**Standalone intelligibility:** arc_close must be watchable by a viewer who finds it first.
arc_pickup (P1) establishes: who this character is, where they are, what the immediate stakes are — all through image and action, no exposition.
